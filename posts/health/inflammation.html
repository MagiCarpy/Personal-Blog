Analysis of Inflammation and Proposed Solutions
Date Published: 11/2/2024

Context

After undergoing a stem cell transplant (allo-HSCT) in response to a diagnosis of Myelodysplastic Syndrome (MDS), I have had to find methods of balancing the benefits and detriments of having a new immune system.

The purpose of the stem cell transplant was to replace my own, cancerous/faulty, immune system (i.e. the monocyte lineage) with a healthy donor chosen from a Global Bone Marrow Registry (NMDP). In my case, the donor was a German woman who was an 11/12 HLA match with myself. Adequate matching is necessary to prevent extensive Graft vs Host Disease (GVHD) but maintain enough of a Graft vs Illness effect so that my cancer would have a lower chance of relapse after the chemotherapy and transplant.

Today, almost a year out from this transplant which occurred December 5th of 2023, I have been somewhat cursed into dealing with a rather overactive immune system. Some issues of this have manifested as a form of lung inflammation, organizing pneumonia, and pneumomediastinum. This has resulted in a prolonged, prescribed course of prednisone, a potent anti-inflammatory.

Solutions

I have looked into natural, nutritional routes of easing these symptoms so that I can potentially wean off any medical grade immunosuppresants and anti-inflammatories. A presumably credible source of health information, Dr. Berg, suggests incorporation of tumeric into the diet. Based on multiple research papers curcumin in tumeric has been shown to have anti-inflammatory effects on auto-immune diseases like rheumatoid arthritis that are compounded when combined with pepper and fats which aid absorption.
