Expose Yourself to Conflict
Date Published: 11/2/2024

Based on prior observation, anecdotal experiences and reading The Art of Thinking Clearly, the following conclusions can be reasonably made.

It is known that human innovation has been predicated upon the interactions of those with conflicting thought processes and beliefs, specifically: "Competition Breeds Innovation". This observation should be quite obvious so let's breakdown a more nuanced application to the everyday life of an individual: the confirmation bias.

The specific reason conflict yields innovation and progress is through mutual "checks" on thought processes between individuals. More often than not, you are unable to control your biases. Similar to how you are incapable of controlling procrastination or unable to watching something that might disprove your already established preconceived beliefs.

Likewise, If you look hard enough for support of something, you will find it. If you look hard enough for a connection between two completely unrelated subjects, you will find it.

The implementation of some sort of trigger is required to break free from these loops of logically unsound beliefs.

Solutions

Just like you would solve procrastination, find a reminder to "jolt" you out of a narrower way of thinking. Train yourself to consciously ask yourself, "Am I honestly considering conflicting information?".

Detach yourself from any preconceived beliefs. Resist handling beliefs like a loyal sports enthusiast aligned wholeheartedly to one team. Unless you are a lawyer whose job of defending the defendant is greatly aided in a self-deluded adherrence to a single presumption of intent in defending the defendant, why attach yourself to a belief? Attachment to a faulty line of fixed thinking ultimately harms yourself. Open yourself to accepting differing but reasonable beliefs and allow flawed beliefs to be discarded.

Exposure to varied opinions, perspectives, and thinking that challenges your assumptions is the key to fully realizing the potential of the two supplementary solutions above. Merely tolerating diverse viewpoints is not enough; actively seek out what might be uncomfortable interactions and surround yourself primarily with: those who think differently than you as well as those who disagree with you.
